Overview

Hylocereus undatus cv. Vietnam White 1

Average brix: Not tested
Average Mass: 519 grams

This is the first variety of dragon fruit is the first I have collected, and so is pretty special to me. As can probably be inferred from the name, this variety is the white-fleshed dragon fruit from Vietnam. This particular cultivar of Vietnam White has a nice sweetness and is not at all bland.

This variety is a medium-paced grower, faster than Common Red but slower than my other cultivar of Vietnam White; Vietnam White 2.

Buds

In my experience, this variety buds from early December (early summer) to late February, although not very consistent as of yet. Buds are typically a pale green colour when they first emerge, and continue to stay green as they mature.

Flowers

Vietnam White 1 has a beautiful, large flower, with a stigma stuck in its anthers. This means this variety will set fruit on its, without hand or cross pollination, so much so that the fruit will still be large.

This variety is known to be a good source of pollen for the Isis Gold cultivar, and will probably work for other varieties, although that is an area I have not yet tested.

Fruit

The fruits of Vietnam White 1 consistently grow to a resonable size above half a kilogram through self-pollination. The bracts don't really turn purple at all on this variety. Wait until the fruits of Vietnam White 1 are just about to split, if not just split, for the best flavour.
