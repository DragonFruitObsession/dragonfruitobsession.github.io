Overview

Hylocereus polyrhizus cv. Common Red

Average brix: 12.5
Average Mass: 554 grams

This variety, as can probably be inferred from the name, is the common red-fleshed variety. It is known to be high in antioxidents, and has a refreshing taste when chilled. Under the right conditions, growth is very thick, and can get up to around 10 centimetres in thickness. Flowers are self-fertile, and probably self-pollinating (untested).

Buds

This variety buds late into the season, sometimes even producing winter fruits. The buds have lots of sap which the ants just go crazy over, although it doesn't seem to stunt the buds too much. As with most red-fleshed varieties, the buds have red tips.

Flowers

This variety has a pretty standard flower when compared to other varieties. The stigma protrudes slightly away from the anthers, however, which could make self-pollination less effective.

Common Red produces abundent pollen which could be used to cross-pollinate other varieties, although that is an area I have not yet tested.

Fruit

The fruits of common red are quite large upon successful pollination. In the ripening process, the tips of the fins turn red first, followed by the rest of the fruit. Like most red-fleshed varieties, the fruits should be left to split before picking for the best flavour.
